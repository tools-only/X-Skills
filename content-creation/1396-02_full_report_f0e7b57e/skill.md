## Introduction and Methodology {#introduction}

### Research Scope

This report examines Michael Jackson's popularity in China during what could be considered his peak period of influence in the Chinese market: from the early 1990s through his death in 2009. This period encompasses:
- The Dangerous album and tour era (1991-1993)
- The HIStory album and tour period (1995-1997)
- The Invincible album release (2001)
- The 2003-2005 trial period
- The final years (2006-2009)

### Research Limitations

**Critical Methodology Note**: This research was compiled during a period when web search tools reached monthly usage limits. As a result:

**Strengths**:
- Comprehensive synthesis of available historical information
- Structured analytical framework
- Clear identification of what requires verification
- Logical progression based on documented historical events

**Limitations**:
- Specific quantitative figures require verification from primary sources
- Exact dates and viewership numbers need confirmation from official archives
- Chinese-language sources could not be accessed directly
- Real-time web searches were unavailable

### Confidence Level System

- ✅ **High Confidence**: Multiple reliable sources, well-documented events
- ⚠️ **Medium Confidence**: Generally accepted historical knowledge, needs specific verification
- ❓ **Low Confidence/Unverified**: Requires primary source documentation

## Album Sales and Distribution {#album-sales}

### Distribution Challenges and Opportunities

#### The Piracy Paradox

**Critical Insight**: Pirated distribution likely expanded rather than diminished MJ's reach in China.

**Why This Matters**:
- **Price Accessibility**: Pirated copies 5-15 RMB vs. 80-150 RMB for official
- **Availability**: Pirated versions available in small cities and towns
- **Market Penetration**: Piracy created widespread awareness
- **Long-term Impact**: Built fan base that later purchased official releases

**Distribution Channels**:

**Official Distribution** (Limited):
- State-approved importers
- Major bookstore chains in big cities
- High-end department stores
- Very limited outside major urban centers
