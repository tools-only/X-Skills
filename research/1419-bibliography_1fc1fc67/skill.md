## VIII. Source Quality Assessment Framework

### Reliability Categories

**A-Level Sources** (Most Reliable):
- Official industry records (IFPI, record companies)
- Government archives (CCTV, state media)
- Academic peer-reviewed publications
- Direct interviews with primary participants

**B-Level Sources** (Reliable):
- Major newspapers and magazines
- Television network records
- Market research reports
- Established news websites

**C-Level Sources** (Useful with Caution):
- Fan websites and blogs
- Secondary reporting (news about news)
- Memoirs and biographies (unless primary source)

**D-Level Sources** (Verify Corroboration):
- Social media posts
- Unverified internet claims
- Anonymous sources

### Source Verification Process

**For Each Claim**:
1. **Locate primary source** if possible
2. **Cross-reference with multiple sources**
3. **Assess source reliability** (A-D rating)
4. **Note any contradictions** between sources
5. **Assign confidence level** (✅, ⚠️, ❓)

**Documentation Standard**:
- **Author/Organization**: Clear attribution
- **Publication Date**: When published
- **Source Title**: Exact title
- **URL/DOI**: Direct link when possible
- **Access Date**: When accessed
- **Confidence Level**: ✅⚠️❓
