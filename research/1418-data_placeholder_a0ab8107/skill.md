## II. Qualitative Data to Be Collected

### A. Interview Question Framework

#### Fan Club Members

**Basic Information**:
- Name: [Pseudonym if preferred]
- Age: [During fandom period]
- Location: [City]
- Years of Fandom: [Years]

**Early Experiences**:
1. When and how did you first discover Michael Jackson's music?
2. What was your first MJ album/song? How did you access it?
3. What made you become a fan?

**Fan Community**:
4. Were you part of a fan club? How did you join?
5. What activities did the fan club organize?
6. How many members were there? How active was the club?
7. How did you communicate with other fans?

**Media and Access**:
8. How did you access MJ's music and videos?
9. Did you watch MJ on television? Which programs?
10. Did you buy official or pirated copies? Why?
11. How did the internet change your fan experience?

**Cultural Impact**:
12. How did being an MJ fan influence your other interests?
13. Did you learn English because of MJ?
14. Did MJ influence your fashion or dance style?
15. How did friends and family react to your fandom?

**Controversies**:
16. How did the 1993 allegations affect you and other fans?
17. How did the 2003-2005 trial affect the fan community?
18. How did Chinese media cover these controversies?

**2009 Death**:
19. Where were you when you heard MJ died?
20. How did the fan community respond?
21. How did Chinese media cover his death?
22. How has your fandom continued post-2009?

**Reflections**:
23. What does MJ mean to you personally?
24. How do you think MJ influenced Chinese popular culture?
25. What would you like people to know about MJ fans in China?

#### Music Industry Professionals

**Professional Background**:
- Position: [Title]
- Company: [Organization]
- Years in Industry: [Years]
- Involvement with MJ: [Specific role]

**Market Observations**:
1. How did MJ's music perform in the Chinese market?
2. What were the biggest distribution challenges?
3. How significant was piracy? How did it affect the market?
4. How did MJ's sales compare with other international artists?

**Business Decisions**:
5. What promotional strategies were used for MJ releases?
6. Were there decisions not to promote MJ more heavily? Why?
7. How did Chinese media respond to promotional efforts?
8. What was the most successful MJ product/release?

**Industry Impact**:
9. How did MJ influence the Chinese music industry?
10. Did MJ's success open doors for other Western artists?
11. What lessons did the industry learn from MJ's popularity?

**Reflections**:
12. What don't people understand about MJ's popularity in China?
13. How should MJ's China experience be remembered?

- Genre: [Type of music/performance]

**Musical Influence**:

## III. Data Quality Assurance

### A. Source Verification Checklist

For Each Data Point:
- [ ] Source identified and accessed
- [ ] Source type assessed (official/secondary/unverified)
- [ ] Publication date confirmed
- [ ] Author/organization identified
- [ ] Direct quotation or exact figure recorded
- [ ] Corroboration sought from additional sources
- [ ] Conflicts between sources noted
- [ ] Confidence level assigned (✅/⚠️/❓)
- [ ] Complete citation recorded

### B. Data Integrity Checks

**Quantitative Data**:
- [ ] Numbers transcribed accurately
- [ ] Units of measurement consistent
- [ ] Time periods clearly defined
- [ ] Estimates clearly marked as estimates
- [ ] Outliers investigated and explained
- [ ] Trends validated with multiple data points

**Qualitative Data**:
- [ ] Quotes transcribed accurately
- [ ] Context preserved
- [ ] Speaker attribution clear
- [ ] Translation accuracy verified
- [ ] Anonymity maintained when requested
- [ ] Personal opinions distinguished from facts

### C. Confidence Level Guidelines

**High Confidence ✅**:
- Multiple independent reliable sources
- Official documentation available
- Direct access to primary sources
- No significant contradictions
- Examples: Official sales reports, CCTV broadcast records, major newspaper coverage

**Medium Confidence ⚠️**:
- Generally accepted but needs specific verification
- Single reliable source
- Secondary reporting of primary sources
- Minor contradictions between sources
- Examples: Fan recollections, industry estimates, general media coverage

**Low Confidence ❓**:
- Unverified estimates
- Single unreliable source
- Significant contradictions
- Requires primary source documentation
- Examples: Uncorroborated sales figures, assumed dates, speculative claims

### Data Backup Plan

**Primary Storage**: Local research drive
**Backup 1**: Cloud storage (encrypted)

**Backup Interval**: Daily during active collection

### Data Privacy and Ethics

**Personal Information**:
- Interviewee names replaced with pseudonyms (unless public figure)
- Contact information stored separately
- Identifying details removed when requested

**Sensitive Information**:
- Unauthorized distribution details reported appropriately
- Industry confidential information protected
- Fan privacy respected

## V. Data Analysis Plan

### Quantitative Analysis

**Descriptive Statistics**:
- Sales totals by album/year/format
- Viewership averages by program type
- Circulation numbers by publication type
- Fan club membership by city/year
- Internet traffic by platform/content type

**Trend Analysis**:
- Sales trends over time
- Viewership changes
- Media coverage frequency
- Fan community growth
- Internet adoption impact

**Comparative Analysis**:
- MJ vs. other international artists
- Official vs. pirated sales
- Different geographic regions
- Different time periods

### Qualitative Analysis

**Thematic Analysis**:
- Identify recurring themes in interviews
- Code and categorize responses
- Identify patterns and outliers
- Develop narrative understanding

**Content Analysis**:
- Media coverage tone over time
- Topic changes and focus areas
- Language and terminology use
- Cultural adaptation and interpretation

**Cross-Analysis**:
- Triangulate qualitative with quantitative findings
- Identify convergence and divergence
- Develop comprehensive understanding
